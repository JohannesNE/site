AU Community of Research Software Users

  • Use open source software?
  • Come to our events!
  • Learn, get support, or help others.

Community building

Meet other people interested in building new skills, sharing their knowledge, and collaborating.

Training sessions

Hands-on and practical learning on (open) software such as R or Python. We run basic to advanced workshops to expand or refine your skills.

Co-working

Share a cup of coffee, beer, or wine. Work together, chat, or get (or give) help. Brilliant ideas do not always come from staring at a laptop in solitude.

Skill sharing

Give and receive. Enjoy the range of skills that other members of the community can contribute and share your own expertise.

Resources and self-learning

Find more material to challenge your skill, keep updated, and learn even more.

Upcoming events

Add the events calendar to your Google account easily by clicking the blue ‘+’ button on the bottom of the calendar. Use Outlook? Check out the instructions on the ‘More Info’ page.

We are always looking for instructors, so please sign yourself up if you are interested. Sessions are subject to change depending on instructor or room availability and any other unforeseen events, but we’ll send out emails about any changes a week before the event.

Tell us what you want from this community by fill out our surveys.

    2-DAY SOFTWARE CARPENTRY WORKSHOP

    Date: Tue, Oct 22, 2019 at 09:00

    Location: Building 5361, room 144, Finlandsgade 27, 8200 Aarhus N

    This workshop aims to help researchers get their work done in less time and with less pain by teaching them basic research computing skills. This hands-on workshop will cover basic concepts and tools, including program design, version control, data management, and task automation. Participants will be encouraged to help one another and to apply what they have learned to their own research problems.

  • R Rhus Advanced

    RHUS FIRST MEETING

    Date: Tue, Oct 29, 2019 at 17:00

    Location: AU Library in Nobelparken, main floor, left from entrance behind glass wall

    The very first Rhus useR meeting in a long time, we are proud to present 2 speakers. The meeting will hopefully end with some light drinks and snacks.

    Using R to fail fast: Driving data acceptance in industry

    Using data in a traditional value based company can be taunting task. In this talk, I will present some of the approaches I used for pushing data tools to my colleagues, and why failing to produce the perfect tools is important.

    Stefan Høj-Edwards, Production Data Analyst, Kamstrup A/S @iysik_com
